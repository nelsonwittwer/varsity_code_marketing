Why Code In Minecraft?

Coding, by itself, can seem daunting and abstract. When you can use something like Minecraft that your child most likely already has a deep understanding and passion for, you remove that layer of abstraction and you get to see immediate results. Best of all, you get to channel their passion for the game to help them develop a skill that will prepare them for a wide variety of careers in technology.

What Programming Language Will Be Taught?

We will be working in the Python language. Python is a very easy to learn language that is also incredibly powerful. It is also the preferred programming language at Google.

Build Your Own Microcomputer

It's Yours To Keep So You Can Keep Learning After The Course

Build A Raspberry Pi Microcomputer

This little computer was designed to introduce people to people of all ages to hardware and software development.

We will be building and configuring these from the ground up, so you'll be able to learn about everything that goes into making a computer work.

Learn Linux, The Operating System That Powers The Web

While you may or may not have heard of it, you use Linux every day. It powers the majority of servers that host websites, apps, and email. Working with Linux will prepare your child to run and operate a server of their own.

Work With Your Raspberry Pi At Home

We want your child to work with their Raspberry Pi at home and continue learning outside of class. The Raspberry Pi has an HDMI output, meaning if you have a TV or computer monitor with an HDMI input, all you'll need is a keyboard and mouse. If you don't have a keyboard and mouse at your home, you can pick both up for around $15 on Amazon.

Course Format

Coding in Minecraft helps your child visualize the abstract nature of code and math.

  • Week 1

    Introduction to your Raspberry Pi and Coding

    Setup your Raspberry Pi, and get a feel for your new coding environment.

  • Week 2

    Introduction to Coding in Minecraft

    Now that we have our computers built, we will explore the possibilities of writing code to build Minecraft structures.

  • Week 3

    Code Logic - Part 1

    We'll learn the basics of conditional code logic to allow us to build more advanced structures.

  • Week 4

    Code Logic - Part 2

    We'll learn how to better compose our code by learning about methods.

  • Week 5

    Code Logic - Part 3

    Extract logic into repetable classes and object oriented programming.

  • Week 6

    Wrap-up And Demo Day

    During the last day of the course, we invite you to come and see your child's growth as our child will present on their favorite programs and accomplishments.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does enrollment cost?

$360 for the 6 week course. The Raspberry Pi microcomputer and accessories that your child will be working with is included inthe cost.

Where will classes be held?

The classes will be held at a residence in Draper, Utah. We'll email you the exact address after you enroll.

I really loved seeing how much progress my child made, do you offer further courses?

That is definitely the plan! Stay tuned for advanced Minecraft classes as well as classes in robotics and web development.

What Pre-requisites Do you Recommend?

The biggested indicator for success is an excitement about the course itself. If your child is excited about learning to code, or exploring new possibilities they will be primed to succeed.

A firm foundation in math will also help out. We will be using variables and plotting in three dimension grids. As long as they are ok learning and working in these concepts they will be fine. This is a great way to help them utilize all that math they never thought they'd actually use :)

Can I take the class with my child?

We have a limited number of seats for each class, so if you are interested in participating with your child, you'll need to enroll for two spots.

Additionally, we'll have the lectures online if you'd like to watch and keep up with your student.

What kind of out of class support do you offer?

If you are stuck, you are more than welcome to email the course instructor to ask for help during the week.

What Will My Child Need to Bring each Week?

Just your Raspberry Pi microcomputer. We will provide everything else.

Will there be Homework?

Yes! Practice makes perfect! We'll have a weekly assignments which will be creative in nature. Each assignment is designed to help yoru child get an understanding of the concepts covered in each class.

Do we get to keep the Raspberry Pi?

Yes! We want to encourage your child to keep learning, long after this course concludes. During the course, we expect your child to use their Raspberry Pi to complete the homework and show off their creations during the start of each class.

How do we use the Raspberry Pi at Home?

The Raspberry Pi comes with an HDMI output. You can hook it up to any TV or Computer Monitor with an HDMI input. If you have a monitor with a DVI input, you could buy a converter and still use it. Contact us and we'll give you pointers on it.

You'll also need a keyboard and mouse. If you don't have an old set laying around, you can pick up both for about $15 on Amazon.

Can We Bring Our Own Computers?

Unfortunately, no. In order to simplify the learning and teaching process, we'd like everyone to work in the same environment. We are really excited about the Raspberry Pi format, as we can also inclucde lessons in computer hardware. Plus, working on a microcomputer is just really cool.

What if We Can't Make one of the Classes?

We will record every class session and post lectures on our website. You'll just have to make sure you are up to date before the next class, as each class builds on the previous class.

I can't make it during this block, will you have future classes?

We plan on doing this for as long as people are interested. Join the mailing list to stay in the loop for future classes.

That price is out my price range, do you have any cheaper options?

After we've done a class or two, I'd like to package the lectures and homework into their own product. You'll be able to buy those videos on their own in the near future. I imagine those videos will run around $100.
